~ tips + tricks ~


Mrs. VanOtteren

*Do what will take the least ammount of time first, so you have a sense of accomplishment

*Set timers for when you want to take a break so you don't procreastinate.

One strategy for breaks is called the
Pomodoro Technique.

I have found success dividing my time 40-on / 10-off, but this is largely to individual discretion.


Sra. Fendig

"I reccomend having a separate paper of a calendar and writing in everything that I'm committed to that has nothing to do with school, like babysitting, or sports, or visiting grandma, or anything that I know is coming up, and then look at any time left, and divide that into preparing for tests and quizzes and all of that. So I have a good balance of family, friends, and school or job."


Mrs. Graham + Ms. Baltz

"Don't procrastinate - it's easy to procrastinate. Create a to-do list of this you need to do to and check off when you have done it. If it's a big assignment - chunk it. Do 'small parts - small parts - small parts' so you're not looking at the big assignment."

Want some advice for how to chunk your schedule? Check here.


Ms. Churchwell

"I always kind of make a list of what needs to happen with dates, and then just every day try to work on bigger projects a little at a time - that way it's such a big thing at the end."
